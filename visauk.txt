============================================================
U.K. Visa Types & Requirements – Updated Guide (Nov 2025)
============================================================

1. Overview of U.K. Visa / Immigration System
-----------------------------------------------
- The UK offers various visa routes: Visitor, Student, Work, Family / Settlement, Post‑Study (Graduate), ILR, etc.  
- In 2025, a major revision to visa fees came into effect on *9 April 2025*.   
- Premium services (Priority, Super Priority) remain in use.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Key 2025‑Specific Updates
------------------------------------------------------------
- *Visa Fee Increase (9 April 2025)*: Across many visa categories.   
- *Skilled Worker / Global Business Mobility: CoS (Certificate of Sponsorship) admin fee increased to **£525*.   
- *Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR): Fee raised from **£2,885 → £3,029*.   
- *Priority / Super-Priority Services*: Fee unchanged — Priority £500, Super Priority £1,000.   

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Visa Types — Detailed Breakdown
------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 *Visitor / Short-Term Visas*  
- *Purpose*: Tourism, business, short study, transit.  
- *Fees (from 9 April 2025)*:  
  - Short up to 6 months: *£127*.   
  - Long (2-year): *£475*.   
  - Long (5-year): *£848*.   
  - Long (10-year): *£1,059*.   
- *Work*: Not permitted on most standard visitor visas.  
- *Document Requirements*: Passport, proof of financial means, travel plan, ties to home country.  
- *Validity / Entries*: Multiple entry possible, depending on visa type.

3.2 *Student Visas*  
- *Routes*:  
  - Full Student Route (degree-level, long-term)  
  - Short-Term Student (English courses, 6–11 months)  
  - Child Student (under 18)  
- *Fees (from 9 April 2025)*:  
  - Student (main + dependents): *£524*.   
  - Short-term student (6–11 mo): *£214*.   
- *Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)*: Depends on visa length; charged per year. (Example from Skilled Worker table: ~£1,035/year for worker)   
- *Requirements*:  
  - Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) / CoS for students  
  - English language test (if required)  
  - Financial maintenance funds  
  - Passport, photos, other identity documents  
- *Dependents*: Possible on many student visas (spouse, children)  
- *Validity*: Duration of course + extension allowed for certain post-study routes  

3.3 *Work Visas*  
- *Skilled Worker Visa*:  
  - Requires a job offer from a licensed UK sponsor.  
  - *Fees (from 9 April 2025)*:  
    - Up to 3 years (outside UK): *£769*.   
    - Over 3 years: *£1,519*.   
    - In‑UK: up to 3 years *£885, over 3 years **£1,751*.   
  - *CoS Admin Fee*: £525 (new) for Skilled Worker / GBM.   
  - *Other Charges*:
    - Priority / Super-priority: +£500 / +£1,000 (unchanged)   
    - Immigration Skills Charge: depends on employer and size. (From Skilled Worker FAQ)   
- *Other Work Visas*:  
  - Health & Care Worker: fees increased.   
  - Global Business Mobility, Scale-up, Innovator Founder: varying new fees.   
- *Requirements*:  
  - Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)  
  - Passport, job offer, salary minimum  
  - English language (if required)  
  - Criminal / background checks (if required)  

3.4 *Graduate / Post‑Study Route*  
- *Purpose*: Allows graduates from UK institutions to stay and work / look for work after their course.  
- *Fee*: Follows “Work Visa” rules depending on route / duration (check Skilled Worker or other relevant visa type)  
- *Rights*: Work full-time, no requirement for specific sponsor (depending on route)  
- *Dependents*: Possible, subject to visa conditions  

3.5 *Family / Settlement Visas*  
- *Types*: Spouse / partner visas, children, parent visas, etc.  
- *ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain)*:  
  - Fee (from 9 April 2025): *£3,029*.   
- *Requirements*:  
  - Proof of genuine relationship (spouse / partner)  
  - Financial requirement (income threshold)  
  - Accommodation requirement  
  - English language (in many cases)  
- *Other*: Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) may be required  

3.6 *Other / Special Visas*  
- *Start‑up / Innovator*: For entrepreneurs; Innovator‑Founder visa fee increased.   
- *Youth Mobility / Global Talent / Other*: Refer to UKVI for specific 2025 rates and rules.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Processing, Biometric & Premium Services
------------------------------------------------------------
- *Processing Times*: Varies by visa route: use UKVI / VFS for current estimates.  
- *Premium Services*:  
  - Priority Service: +£500 (non‑settlement)   
  - Super Priority Service: +£1,000   
- *Biometric Enrollment*: Required for most visa categories  
- *Digital / e‑Visa*: Many visa decisions / status handled via online portals; check UKVI for details  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Risks, Common Refusal Reasons & Tips
------------------------------------------------------------
- *Common Denial Reasons*:  
  - Insufficient finances (sponsorship or personal)  
  - Weak proof of relationship (for family visas)  
  - Incomplete or inaccurate application forms  
  - Failing to meet English language requirement  
- *Tips*:  
  - Use the latest fee table (post April 2025) when calculating costs  
  - Maintain strong documentation (job offer, financial proof, sponsor letters)  
  - Consider priority services if you need faster decisions  
  - Track biometric appointment & keep documentation ready  
  - Understand healthcare surcharge (IHS) commitment
