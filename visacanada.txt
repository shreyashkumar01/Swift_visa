============================================================
Canada Visa Types & Requirements – Updated Guide (Nov 2025)
============================================================

1. Overview of Canada Immigration & Visa System
------------------------------------------------
- Canada offers temporary resident visas (visitor, study, work), as well as *Permanent Residence (PR)* options.  
- Visas / permits are managed by IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada).  
- Important to track IRCC’s current fee schedule and processing times (which may change).

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Key 2025‑Specific Updates
------------------------------------------------------------
- As of 2025, IRCC fee schedule remains in place, but always verify on IRCC official website.   
- Right of Permanent Residence Fee (RPRF) is *CA$ 575* for many PR applications.   
- Biometric fee: CA$ 85 per person (commonly required) (standard in many applications).

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Visa / Permit Types — Detailed Breakdown
------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 *Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa - TRV)*  
- *Purpose*: Tourism, business visits, family visits, transit  
- *Fee*: CA$ 100 for TRV application (or as per IRCC)  
- *Passport Validity*: Must have a valid passport; IRCC recommends checking expiry  
- *Entries / Validity*: TRV can be single or multiple entry — depends on decision  
- *Required Documents*:  
  - Passport / travel document  
  - Proof of financial means (bank statements, employment)  
  - Travel plan / itinerary / invitation letter  
  - Ties to home country (employment, family)  
- *Biometrics*: Likely required (depending on nationality)  
- *Special Conditions*: Overstay and misrepresentation can lead to bans; must apply well in advance  

3.2 *eTA (Electronic Travel Authorisation)*  
- *Purpose*: For visa-exempt nationals traveling to Canada by air  
- *Fee*: CA$ 7 (commonly)  
- *Validity*: Usually valid for up to 5 years or until passport expiry (whichever is earlier)  
- *Limitations*:  
  - eTA does not allow work or study — separate permit required  
  - Must arrive via air (not all entry methods accept eTA)  

3.3 *Study Permit*  
- *Purpose*: To study at a Canadian “Designated Learning Institution” (DLI)  
- *Fee*: CA$ 150 for the permit application  
- *Biometrics*: CA$ 85 (if required)  
- *Financial Requirements*: IRCC requires proof of sufficient funds for tuition + living costs. In 2025, students need to show IRCC‑specified maintenance funds.  
- *Required Documents*:  
  - Acceptance letter from DLI  
  - Proof of funds (bank statements, scholarship)  
  - Passport / travel document + photos  
  - Biometric info (if required)  
  - Letter of explanation / purpose (optional but helpful)  
- *Validity*: Duration of course + possible grace / post-study period  
- *Dependents*:  
  - Spouse / common-law partner: may get an open work permit (if eligible)  
  - Children: can apply for study permit, if conditions met  
- *Risk / Notes*:  
  - Overstaying or not maintaining status can lead to permit cancellation  
  - IRCC may refuse if funds are not well documented  

3.4 *Work Permits*
- *Types*:  
  - Employer‑specific (tied to a job offer)  
  - Open Work Permit (not tied to specific employer)  
  - International Experience Canada (IEC) work permit (youth / exchange)  
- *Fees*:  
  - Employer-specific: CA$ 155  
  - Open Work Permit: CA$ 100  
  - Biometrics (if needed): CA$ 85  
- *Processing Time (2025)*:  
  - In‑Canada: IRCC’s standard ~120 days for many work permit applications  
  - Outside Canada: depends on visa office; many work permits have ~60‑day standard (varies)  
- *Required Documents*:  
  - Job offer / employment contract  
  - Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) if required  
  - Passport / travel document  
  - Biometric information (if required)  
  - Proof of experience / qualifications  
- *Validity*: Depends on the job, employer, permit type  
- *Conditions / Risks*:  
  - Work permit does not equal permanent residency (unless part of a PR stream)  
  - Compliance (work only at permitted employer, valid permit) is critical  

3.5 *Super Visa*  
- *Purpose*: Allows parents / grandparents of Canadian citizens / PRs to visit for extended periods  
- *Fee*: Typically CA$ 100 (application) + medical insurance for visitor (highly recommended)  
- *Validity*: Multiple entry visa (if granted)  
- *Length of Stay*: Up to 2 years per visit, depending on super visa terms and medical insurance  
- *Required Documents*:  
  - Invitation from child / grandchild in Canada  
  - Proof of income / financial support from inviter  
  - Medical insurance coverage for the length of stay (as per IRCC requirements)  
  - Medical exam (IRCC requirement)  

3.6 *Permanent Residence (PR) / Immigrant Visas*  
- *Streams*:  
  - Economic (Express Entry: Federal Skilled Worker, Canadian Experience Class, etc.)  
  - Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)  
  - Family Sponsorship (spouse, parent, child)  
  - Other (Start‑ups, Business, Humanitarian)  
- *Fees (2025)*:  
  - Permanent Residence application + processing: varies by stream  
  - Right of Permanent Residence Fee (RPRF): CA$ 575 (for many PR applications)   
  - Dependent child (on PR application): CA$ 175 (as per IRCC)   
- *Required Documents*:  
  - Passport / travel document  
  - Educational credentials (if required)  
  - Language test results (for some economic streams)  
  - Proof of funds (if needed)  
  - Police / background check  
  - Medical exam  
- *Processing*:  
  - Application via IRCC → decision → visa issuance  
  - Timing depends heavily on category, backlog, and IRCC capacity  
- *Obligations / Responsibilities*:  
  - After PR, there are residency obligations to maintain status  
  - Applicants should understand their stream’s requirements (e.g., work, investment, experience)

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Compliance, Risks & Common Refusal Factors
------------------------------------------------------------
- *Refusal Reasons*:  
  - Insufficient financial proof (for visitor, study)  
  - Incomplete job offer or LMIA (for work)  
  - Weak proof of genuine visit (for TRV)  
  - Not meeting residency / work conditions  
- *Tips for Applicants*:  
  - Be precise and honest in the application and documentation  
  - Maintain strong proof of funds, sponsor letters, employer letters  
  - Apply well in advance of travel / study start date  
  - Keep track of permit expiry and compliance points  
  - Use IRCC’s “Check Processing Times” tool to estimate timelines
