============================================================
Ireland Visa Types & Requirements – Comprehensive Guide (Nov 2025)
============================================================

1. Overview of Ireland Visa / Immigration System  
------------------------------------------------  
- Ireland visas are classified broadly into *short-stay visas (Type C)* and *long-stay visas (Type D)*. 0  
- There are visas for: Visitors / Tourists, Study (Student Visas), Work Permits, Re-entry, and more.  
- Visa applications for non‑EEA / non‑Swiss nationals are handled via Irish Immigration Service Delivery (ISD) or local Irish embassies / VFS, depending on country.  
- Biometric capture (fingerprints + photo) is required for many visa applicants. 1  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Key 2025‑Specific Notes / Updates for Ireland
------------------------------------------------------------  
- For *student visas, in 2025, students typically need to show **€10,000 / year* (or equivalent) for living expenses. 2 3  
- English-language test is required when applying for long-term study visas. 4  
- Visa application fees remain: generally *€60* for a single-entry short visa, *€100* for multiple entry. 5  
- Re‑entry visas (for those who have an Irish Residence Permit) also have defined fees: €60 for single-entry, €100 for multi, emergency application more. 6  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Visa Types — Detailed Breakdown
------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 *Short-Stay Visa (Type C)*  
- *Purpose*: Tourism, business visits, short study (< 90 days), transit.  
- *Fee*:  
  - Single-entry: *€60*. 7  
  - Multiple-entry: *€100*. 8  
- *Passport / Validity*: Passport must be valid; visa validity depends on consulate decision.  
- *Documents*:  
  - Valid Passport  
  - Proof of funds for stay  
  - Travel itinerary / invitation (if any)  
  - Travel / health insurance may be required or asked by visa officer (not always mandatory, but can be requested) 9  
- *Other Conditions*: Visa fee is non-refundable even if the visa is refused. 10  

3.2 *Student Visas (Long Stay, Type D)*  
- *Types*:  
  - Short-term “C” visa for study (e.g., 3‑month courses) 11  
  - “D” visa for courses longer than 3 months / degree-level programs. 12  
- *Fees*:  
  - As per ISD / visa-sponsor source, many students pay ~€60 single-entry, €100 for multi-entry. 13  
  - (Note: some sources for Indian students / particular visa‑offices have slightly different “local currency” equivalent) 14  
- *Financial Requirements*:  
  - Must show *€10,000 per year* for living costs. 15  
  - For certain courses, you may need to pay at least €6,000 of tuition before visa application if total course fee is > €6,000. 16  
- *English Language*: Required where applicable (test like IELTS / TOEFL, per course / school). 17  
- *Work Rights*:  
  - Students on a valid Stamp 2 (or relevant immigration permission) can work up to *20 hours/week during term* and up to *40 hours/week during holidays*. 18  
- *Post-Study / Graduate Rights*: Some students may apply for a “Third Level Graduate Programme” (or similar) to stay and work / search for work after course completion. 19  
- *Documents*:  
  - Letter of Acceptance / Offer from Irish institution  
  - Proof of funds (bank statements, sponsor, scholarship)  
  - Tuition fee payment / deposit proof (if required)  
  - Passport + photos  
  - Visa application form (AVATS system) 20  
- *Registration After Arrival*:  
  - Non‑EEA students must register with Immigration Service Delivery / GNIB (Garda National Immigration Bureau) and get a *GNIB / IRP card*. 21  
- *Medical / Health Insurance*: While not always absolutely required for the visa application, having health insurance is highly recommended. 22  

3.3 *Work / Employment Permits (“Work Visas”)*  
- *Permit Types*:  
  - *Critical Skills Employment Permit* (for highly skilled roles)  
  - *General Employment Permit* (for other jobs)  
  - Intra-company Transfer Permit, Internship Permit, etc. 23  
- *Fees (2025): According to one breakdown, an employment permit application can cost **€1,000* for many of these categories. 24  
- *Minimum Salary (for some permits)*: As per that source, Critical Skills permit often requires a salary around €44,000 annually. 25  
- *Validity*: Depends on permit type (up to 2 years or more; renewal possible)  
- *Passport & Documents*:  
  - Valid passport  
  - Job offer / contract from Irish employer  
  - Proof of qualifications and relevant experience  
  - Application form for the specific employment permit  
- *Dependents / Family*: Some work permit types allow spouse / children to apply for dependent permissions (depends on permit)  
- *Conditions / Risks*: Non‑compliance may lead to permit cancellation; must follow conditions tied to the permit.

3.4 *Re‑Entry Visa*  
- *Purpose*: For non‑EEA nationals who hold an Irish Residence Permit (IRP) and need a visa to re-enter. 26  
- *Fees*:  
  - Single-entry re-entry visa: *€60*. 27  
  - Multiple-entry re-entry visa: *€100*. 28  
  - Emergency application: higher fees (e.g., +€100 admin). 29  
- *Validity*: Multiple-entry re‑entry visa validity is tied to the IRP card’s validity. 30  
- *Documents*:  
  - Passport + IRP card  
  - Evidence that IRP is still valid / in use  
  - Application form + any required photos / attachments  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Passport / Validity and General Requirements  
------------------------------------------------------------  
- Passport should be valid when applying; there’s no fixed “six-month beyond stay” rule publicly mandated, but good practice is to have a reasonably valid travel document.  
- Visa application is mostly done via *AVATS online system* for long-term visas. 31  
- Biometric data (fingerprint + photo) may be required for many visa categories. 32  
- Supporting Documents: vary by visa type — financial proof, job / admission letter, accommodation plan, travel itinerary, etc.  
- Application Fee is non-refundable even if visa is refused / withdrawn. 33  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Processing Times & Logistics  
------------------------------------------------------------  
- Processing times vary by visa office (embassy / consulate) and volume; delays are possible depending on documents / verification needed. 34  
- After arrival (for long-stay / student visas), non‑EEA nationals must register with Irish immigration and get an *IRP / GNIB card*. 35  
- For student visa applicants (especially Indian / Nepal nationals), applying via VFS is common, and AVATS printout + biometrics are needed. 36  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Risks, Common Denial Reasons & Tips  
------------------------------------------------------------  
- *Common Denial / Refusal Reasons*:  
  - Insufficient proof of funds (living + tuition)  
  - Incomplete / inaccurate documentation (acceptance letter, bank statements)  
  - Not meeting English-language requirement (if needed)  
  - Incorrect or missing visa application forms  
- *Tips for Applicants*:  
  - Keep *clear financial documentation*: bank statements, sponsor letters, loan letters.  
  - Pay required tuition / deposit (if applicable) before visa application to strengthen case. 37  
  - Apply well in advance, especially for student visas (account for VFS appointment, biometrics, processing).  
  - Make sure to register with Irish immigration (IRP / GNIB) post‑arrival.  
  - Maintain valid insurance / funds for living cost during stay.
